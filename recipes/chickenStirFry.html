Chicken Stir Fry

Description

Traditionally, Stir Fry is a dish loaded with fresh veggies, a source of protein, and a sauce. It is usually served over rice, as the rice can act as a main carb source.
In this case, we are using chicken as our main protein.
Stir Fry is actually a cooking technique that originates from China at least 1500 years prior to today. It is executed by using a Wok or a large skillet with hot oil to quickly cook food.
Prep Time: 8 minutes
Cook Time: 10 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1lb boneless, skinless chicken breast -- cut up into 1 inch cubes
  • salt (to taste)
  • pepper (to taste)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 yellow bell pepper
  • 1/2 red bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup baby carrots
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 tsp minced ginger
  • 2 minced garlic cloves

    • Stir-Fry Sauce ingredients

  • 1 tbsp corn starch
  • 2 tbsp cold water
  • 1/4 cup low sodium chicken broth
  • 3 tbsp low sodium soy sauce
  • 3 tbsp low sodium soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • 1/2 tsp crushed red pepper flakes

Steps

    Stir-Fry Sauce

  1. In a medium bowl, mix corn-starch and water. Add in the rest of the sauce ingredidents and continue whisking. Set this aside.

    2. Chicken

  2. Add 1 tbsp of olive oil to large skillet/wok over medium-high heat.
  3. Cook chicken in batches for 3-5 minutes or until cooked through thoroughly. Place aside in a bowl or on a plate.

    4. Veggies

  4. Reduce heat to medium, add in 1 tbsp of olive oil to wok and toss in your veggies(bell peppers, broccoli, carrots). Cook until crisp tender. Toss in garlic and cook for another minute.

    5. Combining it all

  5. Add the chicken back into the wok/skillet and stir them together. Whisk your stir fry sauce from earlier and pour into the wok and mix.
  6. Bring it to a boil stirring occasionally and let it boil for 1 minute.
  7. Now plate over rice or noodles!

Pancakes

Description

Pancakes have been ingrained in American history as a breakfast staple, but have been made for years in other savory, sweet ways as well across the world.
Pancakes have been dated back to Ancient Greece in the 5th century BC, but were called tagenites or tagenias, which are all words derived from the greek word for frying pan.
But the Middle English word 'pancake' did not appear until the 15th century.
These will be the traditional american breakfast food that everyone knows and loves. Best served with a little butter and maple syrup!
Prep Time:10 minutes
Cook Time:10 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp white sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 egg beaten

Steps

  1. Combine flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt in a large mixing bowl and whisk until smooth.
  2. Heat a lightly oiled griddle or frying-pan over medium-high heat.
  3. Pour or scoop batter onto griddle for ~1/4 cup serving for each pancake. Cook until bubbles form on top and edges are dry, then flip. Cook until browned on underside. Do this with remaining batter.
  4. Serve hot as a stack on a plate, add butter on top and maple syrup to taste. Enjoy!
